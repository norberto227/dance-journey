Ballet

To start ballet, focus on mastering the five basic positions
and foundational steps,
and practice regularly to build strength and flexibility.

Get Started with ballet

1.Warm-up:Before you begin, it's essential to warm up your
body to prevent injuries. Spend about 5 minutes doing low-impact cardio, such
as walking or jogging in place. Follow this with dynamic stretches to prepare
your muscles for movement.

2.Basic-position: yourself with the five basic
positions of the feet in ballet:

First Position: Heels together, toes turned out.

Second Position: Feet shoulder-width apart, toes turned out.

Third Position: One foot in front of the other, heel
touching the arch of the front foot.

Fourth Position: One foot in front of the other, with a
small gap between them.

Fifth Position: One foot in front of the other, heel touching the toe of the front foot.

Salsa

Counts 1–8: Warm-Up Groove

Get Started with Salsa

Basic Salsa (Partner or Solo) – 8-Count Step

Timing:

Salsa follows this rhythm:

1 – 2 – 3 – (pause) – 5 – 6 – 7 – (pause)
You step on 1, 2, 3 and 5, 6, 7, and pause on 4 and 8.

Leader's Basic Step (Facing Partner)

(If you're dancing solo, follow these directions)

Counts 1–4:

1.Step forward with left foot – Count 1

2.Step in place with right foot – Count 2

3.Step back to center with left foot – Count 3

4.Pause – Count 4

Counts 5–8:

5.Step back with right foot – Count 5

6.Step back with right foot – Count 5

7.Step to center with right foot – Count 7

8.Pause – Count 8

Follower's Basic Step (Reverse Footwork)

Step back with right foot – Count 1

Step in place with left foot – Count 2

Step forward with right foot to center – Count 3Pause – Count 4

Step forward with left foot – Count 5

Step in place with right foot – Count 6

Step back to center with left foot – Count 7Pause – Count 8

K-Pop

Counts 1–8: Warm-Up Groove

Get Started with K-Pop

K-Pop Dance Routine (8-Count, Beginner Level)

Timing:

Most K-pop routines use 8-count phrases (1–8). We'll build
a 32-count short routine with pop attitude and simple steps.

Counts 1–8: Intro Groove

1.Step right to the side, right hand pops up (like a “stop” sign) – Count 1

2. Step left to the side, left hand pops up – Count 2

3.Body roll down (chest to hips) – Counts 3–4

4.Tap right foot forward, snap fingers – Count 5

5. Tap left foot forward, snap fingers – Count 6

6.Pose or hip pop to the right – Count 7

7.Pose or hip pop to the left – Count 8

Heels Dance

Get Started with Heels Dance

Heels Dance Routine (Beginner Level, Step-by-Step, 32-Count)

Section 1: Walk & Pose (Counts 1–8)

Step forward right (hips sway) – Count 1

Step forward left – Count 2

Step forward right – Count 3

Step forward left + pose (hand on hip, head tilt) – Count 4

Slow head roll (right to left) – Counts 5–6

Bend knees slightly, hands slide down thighs – Counts 7–8

ection 2: Hips & Hair (Counts 9–16)

Step side right + hip circle – Count 1

Step side left + hip circle – Count 2

Touch chest, slide hands down to waist (sexy body roll) – Counts 3–4

Hair flip or head whip – Count 5

Walk back (right, left) – Counts 6–7

Pose with hand behind head – Count 8

Section 3: Floor Tease (Counts 17–24)

Slide right leg out to side (lower into squat or lunge) – Count 1

Place right hand on floor – Count 2

Slide into seated or one-knee pose – Counts 3–4

Roll hips or body forward – Counts 5–6

Rise slowly, using thighs/core – Counts 7–8

Open Style Dance

Get Started with Open Style Dance

Open Style Routine (Beginner – 32 Counts, Emotional/Pop vibe)

Section 1: Controlled Groove (Counts 1–8)

Step right to the side + head tilt left – Count 1

Step left to center + shoulder roll back – Count 2

Step left to the side + right hand floats across chest – Count 3

Step right in place + left hand follows – Count 4

Isolate chest forward-back – Counts 5–6

Drop into soft plie (bend knees), arms slide down – Counts 7–8

Section 2: Flow & Expression (Counts 9–16)

Step forward right + right arm swings up – Count 1

Step forward left + left arm follows – Count 2

Pause & hold (breath moment) – Count 3

Step back right + head drops – Count 4

Wave arms outward from chest (like releasing energy) – Counts 5–6

Contemporary Dance

Get Started with Contemporary Dance

Contemporary Routine (32 Counts – Beginner Level)

Section 1: Breathe & Expand (Counts 1–8)

Stand center, arms slowly rise overhead (inhale) – Count 1

Open arms wide, slight back arch (exhale) – Count 2

Step right to the side, arms sweep to right – Count 3

Step left to center, arms float down – Count 4

Body wave (chest to hips) – Count 5

Drop into soft plié, arms to floor – Count 6

Roll up through spine slowly – Counts 7–8

Section 2: Traveling & Reaching (Counts 9–16)

Step forward right, left arm reaches across – Count 1

Step forward left, right arm follows – Count 2

Pause in lunge, deep breath, torso rotates – Count 3

Rise and turn slowly to the right – Count 4

Step right, sweep left leg into a rond de jambe (circle) – Count 5

Step left back, arms extend upward – Count 6

Breaking Dance

Get Started with Breaking Dance

Breaking Routine – Beginner Level (32 Counts)

1. Toprock (Counts 1–8)

Step right foot across left, arms swing open – Count 1

Step left in place, arms return to neutral – Count 2

Step left foot across right, arms swing open – Count 3

Step right in place, reset – Count 4

2. Go-Down (Counts 9–12)

Drop to crouch (right foot leads) – Count 9

Place both hands on floor, right foot kicks out – Count 10

Extend both legs into plank-like position (or crouch) – Count 11

Shift into footwork position (on hands and toes) – Count 12
